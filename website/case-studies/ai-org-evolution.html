05 Case Study

AI Org Evolution

ZalandoHead of Design150+ Person Design Org

In an era of AI hype, we are in early experiments — not defined best practices. We aren't trying to "solve" AI; we are iteratively integrating it to move the needle on designer velocity and business impact.

Hero image placeholder · 16:9 — AI + Design Day or workflow visual

My Philosophy

Experimentation mindset, corporate-scale constraints

Leading a 150+ person design org within a 7k+ company means balancing an experimentation mindset with the hard realities of corporate compliance and organizational scale. Here's a snapshot of what's working, what's breaking, and what I'm excited about next.

Current Wins

Where AI is already moving the needle

  1. 1

    Research Ops at scale

    Deployed LLM-based automations — Guides, Screeners, Insights — to expand the reach of a 2-person ResOps team, significantly increasing the volume and impact of designer-led studies.

  2. 2

    Operational transparency

    Used LLMs to synthesize portfolio updates and cross-departmental newsletters, effectively breaking down silos in a large-scale organization.

  3. 3

    Figma workflow automation

    Integrated AI tooling to automate accessibility (A11Y) documentation and design-system token auditing, reducing manual maintenance time for ICs.

  4. 4

    Upskilling & community

    Chairing our internal "AI + Design Day" conference to bridge the skills gap and foster a culture of shared experimentation.

Hype Meets Reality

The honest friction points

  1. !

    Compliance bottleneck

    Corporate security structures often move slower than the tooling (e.g., Figma MCP / Claude), creating friction in testing new, high-potential workflows.

  2. !

    Design meets front end

    We've started experimenting with designers using Claude Code to bug-fix, but stay cautious around expectations of bug ownership in the long run.

  3. !

    ROI gap

    While some LLM integrations assist in bug-fixing, the overall ROI on certain AI-first design tools (Figma Make, Lovable) remains negligible for complex, end-to-end product work.

  4. !

    Upskilling tension

    Balancing high-velocity project roadmaps with the time needed for AI education remains a real resourcing challenge.
